Macro environment — diagnosis

Why Japanese companies are moving away from India

India's offshore model of "cheap, high-volume, and fragmented" is, at its root, fundamentally incompatible with Japanese software culture. What Japanese clients require is the ability to infer what is needed without being told, to take intrinsic responsibility for quality, and to fill in the ambiguity of a specification independently. The fragmented Silo model of India is the direct opposite of that.

Vietnam's return is being driven by the combination of cost and perceived compliance. China's return reflects a renewed appreciation for technical depth. And AI is now beginning to eliminate the outsourcing demand for routine coding tasks altogether.

What this means for IFT

IFT as a "cheap resource supplier" will lose its reason to exist within three to five years. However, the direction you have been moving toward — selling Japanese products into the Indian market, building a technical organisation capable of operating end-to-end from communications through the full stack — is precisely the right counter-move, and an exceptionally accurate one.

The strategic essence in a single sentence

What you are describing is a complete transformation from being sold to, to being the one who sells.

India's IT outsourcing industry was designed as a provider of cheap labour. Now that AI is taking that work and Vietnam and China are taking that cost position, remaining in that role means a slow death.

The assets IFT holds — your Japan network, the relationships with MDVR, TAKUMI, and CGSemicon, and the cross-layer technical depth that Shaik represents — are not valued in the outsourcing competition. But as a Japan-to-India product partner, they form an exceptionally rare combination.

The Sales and Marketing hire is the first gate that everything else depends on. Engineering can be built. But the person who can read a Japanese client's unspoken expectations and open the doors of Indian enterprise procurement — that individual cannot be manufactured by engineering alone. This single hire determines the velocity of the entire roadmap.
